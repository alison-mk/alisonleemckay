Lessons in letting go


I am sitting on a bench

my favorite bench

texting you

you walk by

outside

looking at your phone

heading the other way

I smile at the coincidence

but it is a precursor

I let you go


Four weeks later

walking down Bellevue Ave

you cross the street up ahead

heading the other way

we miss an encounter by 30 seconds

I say hello in my head

and also goodbye

I watch you walk

onward with purpose

my friend says

“I can’t believe

we’d see him here”

but I can

I let you go


The next week

I see you sitting

on my bench

you are always here now

we talk

for a moment

there is a glimmer

of our honesty

the connection

I found so compelling

and you did not


Later

you walk past

the bar I’m at

I want you to look in

to see me

I want to text you

to run outside

to make eye contact

for just a moment

but

it won’t

change anything

I let you go


The next week

near the bench

you casually ask if

I’m stalking you

you don’t realize

the bench is mine


now

I feel the bench

slipping away too

a casualty

of our flickering

broken

honesty


I shouldn’t have claimed

such a thing

it was never mine


Days later

I will tell you

we can share the bench

knowing

neither of us want to


I will let you go

but not the bench

yet.
