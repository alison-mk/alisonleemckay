election +


Saturday night, election + 4 days

On a dark street

a stranger asks me

Do you live around here

I say I don’t feel comfortable

having this conversation

He apologizes and walks away

but lingers up ahead

and then ends up behind me

Following.

So instead of walk home

I walk to a grocery store

and take refuge under the flourescent lights

After loitering next to the La Croix

for 10 strange minutes

full of

he must be harmless

but harmless strangers

don't linger and follow.

I emerge

and he's nowhere in sight

I make it home safe.



Sunday morning, election + 5 days

I get catcalled.

He asks for directions

which I provide quickly

and then turn to leave

I’m 15 feet away,

halfway across the street

when he gestures to another man

and says can you tell my friend too

Joking, playful

And I keep walking,

crossing to the other side

My generosity does not

go that far

But then he shouts

It’s because we’re black.

No it’s not I shout back.

Well, you’re sexy he calls out

and my departure resumes.


An accusation of racism

Followed by a rebuke

And a new tactic,

sexualize her.

Who is the victim here?


It’s hard to tell

Because we jumped right past

the part where

I quickly passed out directions

and didn’t give him space,

kindness,

energy

Is that because he was black?

I’m not required to give

strangers on the street

my energy.

But I could choose to

give them more kindness.



Tuesday afternoon, election + 7 days

Sitting on a bench

at a cafe

The man to my left

stretches out his arm

along the rim of

our shared bench

It reaches past my backpack,

the barrier between us

that marks the edge

of my personal bubble

His arm lays extended

within a few inches

of my shoulder

Does he know he’s invading

my personal space?

Why does he feel so comfortable

next to me?
